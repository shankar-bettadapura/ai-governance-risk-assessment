# \## Overview

# 

# This project simulates a full AI governance and risk assessment engagement for a fictional mid-size SaaS company (NovaSphere Technologies) offering cloud-based human capital management software. The assessment evaluates three AI-enabled systems against two frameworks — NIST AI RMF 1.0 and ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — and produces a consulting-grade deliverable covering findings, a risk register, and a prioritized remediation roadmap.

# 

# The project was built to demonstrate practical application of AI governance methodology, not just framework awareness. Every finding traces to observable evidence, every risk maps to explicit framework references, and every recommendation is scoped to a named owner and measurable outcome.

# \## Frameworks Applied

# 

# \### NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)

# Used to structure the assessment methodology across four core functions:

# \- \*\*GOVERN\*\* — Policies, accountability structures, and organizational AI risk culture

# \- \*\*MAP\*\* — AI system context, stakeholder identification, and risk categorization

# \- \*\*MEASURE\*\* — Testing, monitoring, bias evaluation, and explainability controls

# \- \*\*MANAGE\*\* — Incident response, vendor risk, treatment planning, and lifecycle controls

# 

# \### ISO/IEC 42001:2023

# Used as the compliance anchor, mapping findings to mandatory clause requirements and Annex A controls. Key clauses in scope include Cl. 5 (Leadership), Cl. 6.1.2 (Risk Assessment), Cl. 8 (Operation), and Cl. 9 (Performance Evaluation).

# \## Key Findings

# 

# | ID | Finding | Severity |

# |---|---|---|

# | F-01 | AI governance roles and accountability undefined; no AI Risk Owner or governance committee exists | High |

# | F-02 | Shadow AI adoption by employees using unapproved LLM tools with no Acceptable Use Policy or DLP controls | Critical |

# | F-03 | No adversarial testing performed against the customer-facing AI assistant (NovaSphere Assist) | High |

# | F-04 | TalentMatch Engine recommendation model has not undergone bias or fairness audit | High |

# | F-05 | Third-party LLM and ML vendor contracts lack AI-specific data processing terms and subprocessor disclosure | High |

# 

# \*\*Overall Risk Posture: HIGH\*\*  

# 5 of 8 identified risks rated High or Critical. Immediate remediation recommended on F-01, F-02, and F-03.

# \## Methodology

# 

# The assessment followed a four-phase approach aligned to the NIST AI RMF core functions:

# 

# 1\. \*\*GOVERN phase\*\* — Reviewed AI-related policies, governance structures, roles, and accountability mechanisms against ISO 42001 Cl. 5 and Cl. 6 requirements.

# 

# 2\. \*\*MAP phase\*\* — Conducted AI system inventory, stakeholder identification, and use case context analysis. Mapped each system to applicable risk categories.

# 

# 3\. \*\*MEASURE phase\*\* — Assessed testing practices, monitoring capability, bias evaluation processes, and explainability controls against NIST AI RMF MEASURE subcategories and ISO 42001 Cl. 8.

# 

# 4\. \*\*MANAGE phase\*\* — Evaluated incident response readiness, third-party AI risk management, and model lifecycle controls.

# 

# Evidence gathering simulated stakeholder interviews, documentation review (policies, contracts, architecture diagrams), and configuration analysis of in-scope AI systems.
